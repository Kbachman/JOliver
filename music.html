My work is primarily about painting itself. For the past 15 years or so I have been working with encaustic paint. I enjoy the act of painting and the physical qualities of the medium. The weight, density, and sculptural aspects of thick wax and resin seem to amplify those qualities for me. They allow both an additive and subtractive approach. I have always been drawn to common objects as subject matter. I like to render the ordinary object not as a still life, but as an iconographical image, elevating their status to that of a revered object. Bio Born in Whittier California in 1958. Grew up and went to school in both Southern and Northern California. Moved to Los Angeles in 1981 and exhibited paintings in various alternative venues. Moved to Seattle in 1989. Exhibited in various alternative venues and galleries, including Room 104, TPN Gallery Walls, The Kurt Lidtke Gallery, Collusion Unlimited, Solomon Fine Art, Artspace, The Sev Shoon Center, and The Battery Street Gallery.
